AN OUTLOOK INTO WORLD HERITAGE SITES
UNESCO Heritage Sites Around The World
World heritage sites are areas that are recognised as having significant cultural, natural or other forms of notable influence. Awarded by the United Nations-affiliated organisation UNESCO, heritage sites are coveted due to the prestige and status it brings to the host nation of the site. The interactable map below shows the location of each site scattered across the world.
From the map, it can be seen that a high number of heritage sites is concentrated in Europe. This is because of several reasons, such as the condition of the site, and the institution, UNESCO, which originated in Europe. Though an Eurocentric bias is not likely an intentional result (Reyes, 2019), UNESCO'S lack of transparency regarding its selection process can lead to unnecessary misunderstandings among nations.


UNESCO heritage sites are commonly divided across three categories, which are cultural, natural or mixed, a combination of both cultural and natural sites. Cultural sites, which celebrate architectural landmarks, usually built by humans, are the most abundant heritage sites.

Richer regions such as Europe and North America contains the biggest number of heritage sites when compared with other major regions in the world. In contrast, less developed regions such as Africa or the Arab states contains smaller numbers of heritage sites. This implies that the chances of a country obtaining acknowledgement of a heritage site could be affected by factors such as financial and economic aspects.
The majority of currently inscribed sites are safe and secure in their host country. However, there are a notable number of sites that are in danger due to reasons such as lack of maintenance, insufficient funding to support the sites, and the possible negligent destruction of sites by irresponsible parties. Due to this, there have been a number of sites where their status has been revoked over the years.
